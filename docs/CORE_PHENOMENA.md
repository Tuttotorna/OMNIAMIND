## Purpose

This document defines the core pre-output phenomena tracked by OMNIAMIND.

These phenomena are not semantic categories.
They are not psychological states.
They are not claims about cognition.

They are structural targets for measurement.

OMNIAMIND is an upstream diagnostics layer.
Its role is to describe how candidate structural trajectories behave before final output emission.

```text
Dual-Echo -> OMNIAMIND -> OMNIA -> OMNIA-LIMIT -> external decision layer

Within this chain:

OMNIAMIND measures pre-output structural dynamics

OMNIA measures post-hoc output stability under controlled transformations

OMNIA-LIMIT certifies structural saturation / stop conditions

General measurement assumptions

OMNIAMIND assumes access to a sequence of pre-output candidate structural states.

These states:

do not need semantic interpretation

do not need to correspond to human-like thoughts

only need to be comparable as evolving structural candidates


The framework is agnostic about the internal substrate.

It only requires that some notion of candidate-state comparison exists.


---

1. Split

Definition

A split is the emergence of multiple candidate trajectories from a previously more unified structural path.

It marks the beginning of downstream divergence inside pre-output dynamics.

What it is not

A split is not:

a semantic ambiguity

a human-like hesitation

a decision by the system

an error by definition


It is only the structural appearance of branching candidate paths.

Why it matters

A split indicates that a single coherent path is no longer sufficient to describe the ongoing pre-output dynamics.

This matters because later instability may already be latent at the moment branching appears, even if final emission still looks stable.

Possible future measurement target

A future metric may estimate split through quantities such as:

number of distinguishable candidate branches

average pairwise distance between branches

branch dispersion growth rate

persistence of branch separation across steps


Minimal interpretation

High split intensity means stronger branching of candidate dynamics. Low split intensity means candidate dynamics remain more unified.


---

2. Micro-drift

Definition

Micro-drift is the accumulation of small trajectory deviations that do not yet appear as visible instability at the level of final emitted output.

It is sub-threshold divergence.

What it is not

Micro-drift is not:

full collapse

visible output failure

semantic contradiction by itself

noise in the trivial sense


It refers to small structural deviations that remain locally compatible while progressively altering the trajectory.

Why it matters

A system may remain apparently stable while its internal path is slowly departing from earlier structural coherence.

Micro-drift matters because late-stage fragility may originate from a long accumulation of weak deviations rather than from a single sharp break.

Possible future measurement target

A future metric may estimate micro-drift through quantities such as:

cumulative deviation from a local coherence baseline

incremental path curvature

short-horizon divergence integral

deviation accumulation per step


Minimal interpretation

High micro-drift means the path is slowly deforming before visible breakdown. Low micro-drift means local coherence remains more stable over time.


---

3. Echo Persistence

Definition

Echo persistence is the degree to which a prior trajectory continues to influence later candidate states after partial or apparent displacement.

It is residual structural carryover.

What it is not

Echo persistence is not:

memory in a psychological sense

semantic recall

intentional reconsideration

explicit recurrence by definition


It is only the measurable persistence of earlier structural traces inside later candidate dynamics.

Why it matters

A candidate path may appear abandoned while still exerting downstream influence.

This matters because unresolved prior structure may continue shaping the later trajectory, increasing instability, delayed bifurcation, or false reconvergence.

Possible future measurement target

A future metric may estimate echo persistence through quantities such as:

cross-step structural similarity

lagged trajectory correlation

residual branch influence across later states

persistence half-life of prior path signatures


Minimal interpretation

High echo persistence means earlier paths continue to affect later dynamics. Low echo persistence means prior paths decay rapidly after displacement.


---

4. Reconvergence

Definition

Reconvergence is the return of previously diverging candidate trajectories toward a more unified structural alignment before final emission.

It is the opposite movement of branching.

What it is not

Reconvergence is not:

proof of correctness

semantic agreement

restoration of the original path in a strong sense

guaranteed stability


A system may reconverge late without recovering deep structural robustness.

Why it matters

Reconvergence is central because not every divergence leads to failure.

Some pre-output branching structures re-align sufficiently before emission. The ability, speed, and degree of reconvergence may distinguish recoverable instability from non-recoverable instability.

Possible future measurement target

A future metric may estimate reconvergence through quantities such as:

distance reduction among branches over time

convergence slope near emission

branch compression rate

terminal coherence restoration index


Minimal interpretation

High reconvergence means diverging paths return toward structural alignment. Low reconvergence means branch separation remains unresolved.


---

5. Bifurcation Pressure

Definition

Bifurcation pressure is the degree of structural pressure pushing the system toward mutually incompatible downstream trajectories.

It describes instability before or during branching, not just branching after it has already stabilized.

What it is not

Bifurcation pressure is not:

a semantic conflict

a human-like doubt

a final branch count

a decision threshold by itself


It is a structural condition indicating increasing instability of trajectory unification.

Why it matters

A system may display pressure toward incompatible continuations even before a clean split is fully visible.

This makes bifurcation pressure useful as an earlier warning signal than downstream divergence alone.

Possible future measurement target

A future metric may estimate bifurcation pressure through quantities such as:

instability in relative candidate dominance

branch-competition volatility

local sensitivity to small perturbations

incompatibility growth among top candidate paths


Minimal interpretation

High bifurcation pressure means the system is being pushed toward incompatible continuations. Low bifurcation pressure means the path remains structurally more consolidated.


---

6. Collapse Threshold

Definition

The collapse threshold is the critical region beyond which reconvergence among diverging candidate trajectories becomes structurally unlikely, negligible, or impossible.

It marks the transition from recoverable divergence to effectively irreversible branch separation.

What it is not

Collapse threshold is not:

a semantic error boundary

a factual truth boundary

a hard-coded software rule

a universal constant


It is a structural threshold that must be estimated relative to the dynamics being measured.

Why it matters

This is the key boundary for any possible early-abort logic.

If collapse-threshold proximity becomes measurable, then a system may detect that continued generation is unlikely to restore coherent structural alignment.

This does not automatically imply intervention. It only creates the possibility of justified upstream gating.

Possible future measurement target

A future metric may estimate collapse-threshold proximity through quantities such as:

reconvergence probability decay

persistent branch separation beyond recovery range

irreversibility indicators

distance-to-non-recoverable divergence boundary


Minimal interpretation

High collapse-threshold proximity means the system is approaching non-recoverable divergence. Low collapse-threshold proximity means reconvergence remains structurally plausible.


---

Relations among phenomena

These six targets should not be treated as isolated.

A typical structural sequence may look like this:

1. candidate path begins unified


2. micro-drift accumulates


3. bifurcation pressure increases


4. split becomes visible


5. earlier paths remain active through echo persistence


6. either reconvergence occurs


7. or collapse-threshold proximity rises until recovery becomes negligible



This sequence is not mandatory. Real dynamics may skip stages, overlap stages, or exhibit loops.

The purpose of the sequence is only to clarify structural relations among targets.
